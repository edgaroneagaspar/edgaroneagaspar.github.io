Research

Questions, alternatives, discourse structure.

The research page should present a compact map of the main lines of work, not an exhaustive administrative project list.

Line 01

Questions in grammar and discourse

Work on questions, potential questions, inquiry termination, embedded questions, answerhood and the way discourse is organized around issues under discussion.

Line 02

Information structure

Research on focus, topics, alternatives, exhaustivity and the interaction between syntactic form and pragmatic interpretation.

Line 03

Grammar–discourse interface

Work on appositives, anaphora, conditionals, rhetorical relations, discourse referents and how grammatical structure constrains discourse update.

Line 04

Narrative suspense

Interdisciplinary work on suspense as a discourse-driven and question-based phenomenon, connecting linguistics with literary theory and experimental methods.

Line 05

Variation and diachrony

Work on differential object marking, linguistic geography, historical change and the grammaticalization of discourse-related distinctions.

Line 06

Experimental approaches

Experimental studies on exhaustivity, focus, scalar implicatures, truth, evidence, sign language anaphora and real-time measurement of suspense.
